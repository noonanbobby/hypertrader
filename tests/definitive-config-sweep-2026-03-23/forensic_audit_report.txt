     Location: realistic_sim.py lines 150-155 (calculate_market_impact)
     Severity: CATASTROPHIC — root cause of the fantasy results

     The sigmoid adjustment adds a HUGE volatility-based floor for
     trades with participation < 0.5% of daily volume, then DROPS
     TO ZERO for trades between 0.5% and 20%. This creates a 27x
     cliff at the 0.5% threshold:

       $15.03M notional (0.501% partic):  0.57 bps impact
       ← Adding $60K to your order REDUCES impact by 96%

     Result: The model charges LESS for a $500M order (2.1 bps) than
     for a $1.25M order (15.3 bps). Impact is INVERTED above $15M.

       $125M:     1.2 bps  (sigmoid OFF)
       $500M:     2.1 bps  (sigmoid OFF: cheaper than $1.25M!)

     tiny orders, but the threshold (0.5% of daily volume = $15M on BTC)

  UNREALISTIC ASSUMPTIONS:
  ════════════════════════

  1. SINGLE-BAR EXECUTION
     A $500M position enters and exits in a single 15-minute bar.
     In reality, a $500M position would take hours or days to build
     using TWAP algorithms. During this time, the market would move
     adversely (information leakage, price impact persistence).

  2. NO MARKET FEEDBACK / OBSERVER EFFECT
     Signals are computed once at $125 flat sizing, then applied at
     $500M. A $500M position would itself move the market, changing
     the very signals that triggered the entry. The strategy's edge
     may disappear at large sizes because YOU become the market.

  3. STATIC LIQUIDITY
     The model uses rolling 20-bar average volume to estimate daily
     volume. But volume is not constant — it's lower during your
     exit (when you're a forced seller) and higher during panic
     events. The model doesn't account for liquidity withdrawal.

  4. NO COUNTERPARTY RISK
     At $500M notional, the exchange itself becomes a risk. Auto-
     deleveraging, socialized losses, insurance fund depletion — none
     are modeled.

  5. CONSTANT VOLATILITY IN IMPACT
     The impact model uses spot volatility at entry time. But your
     large order itself increases volatility, which should increase
     your own impact (feedback loop).


  ROOT CAUSE OF $1.6B RESULT:

  Three bugs compound multiplicatively:

  1. The sigmoid bug (#1) means that once positions exceed $15M
     (which happens after ~15-20 winning trades from $25K), the
     market impact DROPS from ~15 bps to ~1 bps. From that point
     on, each trade gets nearly the FULL price return with almost
     zero impact cost.

  2. The position limit (#3) allows the account to deploy $500M
     per trade. At 50% equity / 10x leverage, this cap is reached
     when equity hits $100M (BTC) or $40M (ETH) or $10M (SOL).
     Beyond this, growth becomes linear at ~$500M notional per
     BTC trade.

  3. With 155 winning trades at 7.25% average return on notional,
     and impact of only 2 bps per trade, the net return per trade
     at $500M is: 7.25% - 0.04% - 0.07% - 0.94% ≈ 6.2% of
     $500M = $31M per trade. Over ~80 trades at cap, that's $2.5B
     in gross P&L. Even with losses, reaching $1.6B is expected.

  The sigmoid bug is the SMOKING GUN. Without it, the impact model
  would at least charge 15 bps on every trade (still too low, but
  10x better). With the bug, trades above $15M pay essentially
  nothing in impact.
